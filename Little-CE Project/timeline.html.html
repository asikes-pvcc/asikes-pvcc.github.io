Timeline of the Case

June 3, 1961

Burglary at the Bay Harbor Pool Room in Panama City, Florida. Witnesses report seeing Charles Gideon at the scene with items matching those stolen in his pockets and he is arrested

August 4, 1961

Gideon is denied court appointed defense counsel as his was not a capital one

August 26, 1961

Gideon sentenced to five years prison time by a Florida circuit court

January 5, 1962

Charles Gideon submits a Writ of Certiorari to the Supreme Court, requesting they hear his case

April 9, 1962

Florida's Attorney General files a brief in opposition of the Certiorari

June 4, 1962

The Supreme Court grants Gideon's Certiorari and agrees to hear the case

June 22, 1962

: Abe Fortas, an attorney who previously defended victims of McCarthyism and later went on to become an associate justice of the Supreme Court, is appointed as Gideon’s legal counsel

January 15, 1963

Oral arguments (lasting 2.5 hours) in Gideon v Wainwright take place in front of the Court

March 18, 1963

The Supreme Court, finding that the right to counsel is an integral part of the fair trial guaranteed by the Sixth Amendment, reverses Gideon’s conviction

May 15, 1963

Florida’s Supreme Court puts Charles Gideon back on trial, this time represented by counsel

August 5, 1963

Gideon is found not guilty
